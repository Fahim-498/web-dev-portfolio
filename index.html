Trees are essential living organisms that play a vital role in maintaining ecological balance. They provide oxygen, store carbon, stabilize the soil, and give shelter to wildlife. With their roots, trunks, branches, and leaves, trees form forests that act as the Earth’s lungs. Beyond their environmental importance, trees also offer fruits, wood, medicine, and beauty to the world. They reduce air pollution, cool the environment, and contribute to human well-being. Planting and protecting trees is one of the simplest yet most powerful ways to help our planet.
